The bagasse process

The journey from a field of swaying sugarcane to a sustainable dish begins by gathering the leftover bagasse fibres after harvest. These natural materials travel to our processing factory, where they're refined into sturdy sheets before being moulded by heat into a finished product — and, once your meal is finished, returned to a compost heap where they naturally dissolve back into the earth.

A better substitute, by design

Being a waste product of sugarcane, items made from bagasse are a relatively better sustainable substitute than conventionally non-biodegradable, non-renewable disposables that usually include plastic and styrofoam.

Renewable Resource Reduced Waste Lower Carbon Footprint Compostable

In general, regarding the use of bagasse products for disposables, they may be more sustainable nonetheless — the key is ensuring they are sustainably and responsibly produced. That's the standard our facility is held to on every run.
